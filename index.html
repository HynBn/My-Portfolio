Kurt-Tucholsky-Oberschule (KTO)

The Kurt-Tucholsky-Oberschule (KTO) in Berlin-Pankow is an integrated secondary school with a gymnasiale Oberstufe, offering pathways up to the Abitur. With around 900 students, it emphasizes music, arts, cultural education, and bilingual learning, and has been recognized as a Referenzschule Kultur. The school is also a “Schule ohne Rassismus - Schule mit Courage”, reflecting its democratic and inclusive values. Spread across two modernized campuses with renovated outdoor spaces, KTO provides a vibrant learning environment enriched by partnerships with cultural institutions and a wide range of extracurricular activities.

Hochschule für Technik und Wirtschaft (HTW), Berlin

University of Applied Sciences, Berlin

Hochschule für Technik und Wirtschaft (HTW), Berlin is the city's largest public university of applied sciences, founded in 1994, with about 15,000 students across its Treskowallee and Wilhelminenhof campuses. It offers around 80 practice-oriented Bachelor's and Master's programs in fields like engineering, computer science, business, design, and more, emphasizing applied, interdisciplinary research and global partnerships.

My course of study: Internationaler Studiengang Medieninformatik (B.Sc.) or International Media and Computing at HTW Berlin is a six-semester full-time program taught in German and partly in English at the Wilhelminenhof campus. It combines a solid foundation in computer science with media theory and media economics, and offers three specialization tracks: Game Technology & Interactive Systems, Visual Computing, and Web Technology. A key feature is a mandatory minimum 16-week internship abroad, emphasizing the program's international and practical orientation. Graduates earn a Bachelor of Science degree.
